Pottsville, Pennsylvania

THE MINERS
1928 Hotel · Venue · Spa

A Cass Gilbert Masterpiece. Reborn.

In 1828, at the dawn of America's coal age, a bank opened its doors in a young Pennsylvania borough. One hundred years later, the nation's greatest architect built it a cathedral. Now, nearly two centuries after it all began, The Miners 1928 opens a new chapter — one written in marble, brass, and light.

The Story

Two Centuries in the Making

Est. 1828

The Founding

The Miners National Bank opened its doors on October 28, 1828 — just eight months after Pottsville was incorporated as a borough. It predates D.G. Yuengling & Son, America's oldest brewery, by a full year. For an entire century, it anchored the financial life of Pennsylvania's anthracite coal country, serving the miners, merchants, and industrialists who built one of the most consequential regions in American history.

Built 1927

The Architect

For the bank's centennial, its directors commissioned Cass Gilbert — the architect of the Woolworth Building (then the tallest in the world) and the United States Supreme Court. Gilbert brought to Pottsville the same ambition and artistry he gave to Washington and New York. He created something extraordinary: a neoclassical temple to commerce that was, in truth, a love letter to the region it served.

The Details

Every Surface Tells a Story

  • An exterior of Harvard brick crowned by four Doric columns of white Georgia marble
  • Keystones carved with the faces of Mercury and Pluto — guardians of commerce and the deep earth
  • Ten-foot brass doors by sculptor John Polachek, depicting the miners who built this region's wealth. His work resides in the Smithsonian.
  • Inside: a banking hall with a forty-foot vaulted ceiling and a glass-and-bronze skylight that floods the space with natural light
  • Walls of Jeanne d'Arc limestone from France. Floors of Roman travertine — quarried from the same hills that gave stone to the Colosseum.
  • Counters of American black walnut with dark green marble. Fourteen varieties of marble in total.
  • A vault sealed by a 36-ton door — 27 inches of tool-proof steel, locked by 24 bolts and four time mechanisms

Visit

Pottsville, Pennsylvania

Heart of Schuylkill County. The capital of Pennsylvania's anthracite region. A city of remarkable firsts.

Yuengling Brewery

America's oldest operating brewery, founded in 1829. Free tours and tastings just blocks from our door. A Pottsville institution for nearly two centuries.

Mahantongo Street

One of Pennsylvania's most beautiful residential streets. Grand Victorian mansions built by coal barons line both sides — a living museum of 19th-century ambition and architecture.

Schuylkill River Trail

Miles of scenic walking and cycling through the landscapes that shaped this region. From gentle riverside paths to mountain overlooks, the trail connects Pottsville to the countryside.

A City Built on Firsts

First anthracite iron furnace in America (1839). First steam locomotive on the Reading Railroad (1831). The first region to fuel a nation's industrial revolution — and now, a destination being reborn.
