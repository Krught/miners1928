## Building History

### The Miners National Bank
- **Founded:** October 28, 1828 (one of the earliest banks in the anthracite region)
- **Location:** 120 S. Centre Street, Pottsville, Pennsylvania 17901
- **Original charter as national bank:** December 24, 1864 (Charter #649)
- **Current building dedicated:** November 7, 1927 (centennial celebration)
- **100th Anniversary:** November 28, 1928

### Context
- Pottsville became a borough ~8 months before the bank opened
- John Pott founded Pottsville 12 years prior
- Necho Allen discovered coal on Broad Mountain ~40 years before the bank opened (1790s)
- The bank predates D.G. Yuengling & Son brewery by one year (Yuengling est. 1829)
- Bank operated continuously until merger/acquisition chain: Miners National Bank → Heritage National Bank (1995) → Sovereign Bank → Santander Bank (2013) → closed March 2023

### Architect: Cass Gilbert (1859-1934)
One of America's foremost architects. Major works include:
- Woolworth Building, New York City (1913) - world's tallest building at the time
- United States Supreme Court Building, Washington D.C. (1935)
- Detroit Public Library
- Minnesota State Capitol
- Alexander Hamilton U.S. Custom House, NYC

### Exterior Architecture
- Harvard brick construction
- Four Doric columns of white Georgia marble
- Colonial and Greek architectural elements combined
- Keystones above three-story arched windows featuring busts of:
  - Mercury (god of trade and commerce)
  - Pluto (god of mines and quarries)
- 10-foot-tall twin brass entrance doors depicting coal miners at work
  - Designed by sculptor John Polachek (Hungarian-born, 1927)
  - Cost: $10,000
  - Polachek's work is in the Smithsonian Institution's National Portrait Gallery
  - He went on to found General Bronze Corporation in New York City

### Interior Architecture
- 50-foot vaulted ceiling with glass and bronze skylight
- Twelve hanging chandeliers (removed decades ago, being restored)
- **Walls:** Jeanne d'Arc stone (a French limestone of varied color)
- **Floors:** Roman travertine (Italian stone used in the Colosseum)
- **Countertops:** American black walnut topped with dark green marble
- **14 types of marble** used throughout the building
- **Vault door:** 36 tons, tool-proof steel, 27 inches overall thickness
  - 24 steel bolts, 4 inches in diameter
  - Controlled by 4 time locks with electric alarm system

### Bank Presidents (selected)
- John Shippen (1865-1881)
- Jacob S. Ulmer (1904-1927)
- James Archbald (1928-1935)

### Bank Note History
- Total issued: $9,620,900 (1864-1935)
- Total notes: 763,661

## Pottsville & Schuylkill County Context

### History
- Founded by John Pott
- Rose to prominence through anthracite coal industry
- Known as "City of Firsts":
  - First anthracite furnace (1839)
  - First steam locomotive on the Philadelphia and Reading Railroad (1831)
  - First commercial use of anthracite coal for iron smelting
- County seat of Schuylkill County since 1851
- Diverse immigrant population: Irish, German, Welsh, Eastern European
- Grand Victorian homes on Mahantongo Street built by coal barons still stand

### Local Attractions
- **Yuengling Brewery** - America's oldest operating brewery (est. 1829), free tours
- **Schuylkill County Historical Society** - Victorian mansion with mining exhibits
- **Schuylkill River Trail** - Walking/biking trails
- **Mahantongo Street** - Historic coal baron mansions
- **Sharp Mountain** - Where anthracite coal was first discovered (1790)
